The Problem

The time and stress associated with making flashcards often causes people to avoid them, even though they know flashcards have been helpful in the past.

#left 50%?

Background

I live in a college town and worked at a popular study spot and rarely observed anyone using electronic flash card apps.

Hypothesis

Current flashcard apps aren’t adequately addressing the needs of the users, demonstrated by their apparent lack of use.

\#right 50%?
# left 50%?

Validating my asumptions

interviews

5 participants were interviewed
Ages 16 - 30
4 males and 1 female
60% had used an electronic flashcard alternative
All maintained an A-B grade average

#right 50%

Interview participants identified lack of time and stress as their main pain points.
They also expressed that perfectionism, difficulty transporting, and the options to add audio and pictures were also important

Quizlet, Brainscape, and Memrise were the best performing apps on the market but they didn’t meet all the needs that were expressed during interviews

Takeaways

Some participants would either hesitate when clicking the “store decks” button due to the “new cards” button’s close proximity.
The click area to edit cards should be expanded to the full size of the parent card.
Participants expressed confusion about the wording of navigation options.
The card creation screen had a confusing hierarchy that directed the user to retrace their step to complete a task.

Implementing Design Canges

I moved the new card button and navigation to the bottom of the screen to allow for a more natural flow when creating cards.

I clarified the wording of the navigation by increasing the visibility and click size to edit cards.

Future Steps

Test the changes made based on user feedback from the last round of testing.
Conduct additional testing to ensure robust and easy to use search and filter.
Research gamification to incentivize searchable naming conventions.
