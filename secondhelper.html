Identifying the Problem Space

We chose to focus on the non-profit sphere of Indianapolis.
To narrow our scope we conducted a series of interviews with a variety of nonprofits servicing the area, and decided to focus on foodbanks.
We determined that our combined skills and expertise would best put to use assisting Second Helpings, a prominent Indianapolis foodbank.
We worked closely with the client to create an app to assist with food rescue, enabling the reallocation of previously waisted food to those who need it.

Barriers to success

The older drivers make the introduction of new technologies more difficult.
Concerns over food safety and quality severely limit the donations that can be received.
Second Helpings has limited resources to invest in new technologies.

Opportunities

Second Helpings provides their employees with GPS-capable smartphones.
Pen and paper were the primary method for documentation, opening the door for digitalization.
The workers of Second Helping are friendly and willing to adapt.

Who Do These Problems Affect?

The rescue team, consisting of the drivers and the acquisition director, have different tasks that rely on seamless collaboration.
In order to better understand and empathize with both sides of the rescue team, I spent a day shadowing workers.
